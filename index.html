About Me

Hello There,
I'm Litralson (he/him), the first of his name, as far as I know, and please don't ask me its meaning. I graduated from the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru with a Bachelor's and Master's in Biology (BS-MS), which was a research-oriented interdisciplinary program. I'm currently interested in emergent properties in biological systems like pattern formation (Reaction-Diffusion systems), collective behaviour, intelligence, and the origin of life. I like photographing life around me (mostly insects and occasionally humans). I also appreciate well-thought-out designs, whether in information design, architecture, institutional structure, or consumer products.

Projects

  • Emergent Slime Mold Networks for Urban Planning: Through this project, I tried to replicate the work of A. Tero et al 2010 where they showed the networks formed by slime molds (Physarum polycephalum) are similar in efficiency, cost, and fault tolerance to that of the Tokyo rail network in the context of Bengaluru. This was developed with TIFR-ICTS for the Sci560 exhibition season at Science Gallery Bengaluru.

    • The talk given at Science Gallery Bengaluru to train the Mediators. Picture Credits: Disha Kuzhively
  • Biophysics & Molecular Dynamics Simulations: I worked on large-scale molecular dynamics simulations of protein-membrane systems that aimed to improve our understanding of macroautophagy, a process that helps to remove waste materials from cells.
  • Simulations, Cell Biology, & Super-resolution Microscopy: I used Super-Resolution Microscopy (STORM) to map out the Notch-1 distribution on a cell membrane and quantified its properties. Then we simulated the Notch-1 distribution on a cell membrane using a reaction-diffusion model and analysed how that affected the signalling dynamics.
